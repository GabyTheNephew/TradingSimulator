Search & Discovery

Search Bar

This is your starting point. You can type a company's ticker symbol (a unique series of letters representing the stock, like AAPL for Apple or TSLA for Tesla) to pull up its real-time market data and begin analyzing its performance.

Chart Controls & Indicators

Chart Range

This decides how far back in time you want to look on the chart. You can zoom out to see the overarching trend over a Full Year, or zoom in to just see Today's market action.

Candle Timeframe

This sets how much time each visual block (candle) on the chart represents. For example, if set to '1 Day', each shape compresses an entire day of trading into a single readable block. It groups thousands of tiny price movements from that period into one consistent visual structure.

Chart Type

This changes how the price history is drawn, allowing you to choose the visual style that fits your needs:

  • Candlestick: Shows the most detail, including price extremes reached during that timeframe (via the "tails" called wicks).
  • Line: Connects only the closing prices to provide a cleaner, simpler view of the overall trend.

Moving Averages (SMA & EMA)

These lines "smooth out" chaotic daily price jumps to show you the true direction of the trend. They are calculated by averaging past prices over a set period.

  • SMA (Simple Moving Average): Calculates a basic mathematical average of previous prices.
  • EMA (Exponential Moving Average): Similar to SMA, but gives more importance to recent prices. This makes the line react much faster to breaking news or sudden market shifts.

Bollinger Bands

This creates a visual "highway" for the price to help you measure volatility (how wildly the price is fluctuating).

  • If the price touches the upper band, the stock might be overbought (potentially too expensive at the moment and due for a drop).
  • If it hits the lower band, it might be oversold (potentially at a discount and due for a bounce).

Daily Statistics

Open, High, Low, Prev. Close

This is the daily snapshot, which tells the story of the current trading session:

  • Open & Prev. Close: 'Open' is the price when the market started this morning, while 'Prev. Close' is yesterday's final price (used as a benchmark to calculate today's gain or loss).
  • High & Low: Represent the absolute highest and lowest prices someone was willing to pay during the current session.

Volume

Shows the total number of shares that have changed hands today. High volume indicates strong interest from big investors, hype, or panic selling. A price move on high volume is generally considered more significant than a move on low volume.

Executing Trades

Balance

Represents the settled cash available in your account that you can use to buy stocks. You cannot spend more money than what is displayed here.

Bid & Ask Quotes

These are the live market prices dictating what a stock is currently worth:

  • Ask: The lowest price a seller is willing to accept. This is the price you pay when you BUY shares.
  • Bid: The highest price a buyer is offering. This is the money you get when you SELL shares.

Quantity & Estimated Total

You enter the exact number of shares you want to trade. The 'Estimated Total' field multiplies this quantity by the current Ask price to show you a quick on-screen estimate of your expected cost.

Order History Table

Order Status & Details

This acts as a receipt of your past actions. It shows if you bought or sold (Side) and how many shares the market successfully processed for you (Filled Qty).

  • Pending Status: Your order is waiting in the queue because your desired price or quantity isn't available yet. Once the trade goes through, it transitions to PartiallyFilled or Filled.

Current Positions Table

P/L (Profit & Loss) and Value

Provides a real-time valuation of the investments you currently hold:

  • Market Value: How much cash you would receive if you sold all your shares this very second.
  • Total P/L: Compares your Avg Cost (the average price you paid to buy) against the current market price, showing exactly how much money you have gained (in green) or lost (in red).
